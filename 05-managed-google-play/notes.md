# 05 — Managed Google Play & App Distribution

## Business Problem
Employees install unauthorized apps on work devices 
— introducing malware, data leakage risks and 
compliance violations.

IT needs full control over what apps employees 
can access and install on managed devices.

## Solution
Configured Managed Google Play to create a 
controlled corporate app catalog — employees 
can only install IT-approved applications.

## What Managed Google Play Enables
- Silent/automatic app installation on devices
- Granular app update controls
- Custom corporate app catalog
- Employees use work apps without personal 
  Play Store account
- Prevents unauthorized app installation

## Account Types

| Type | Best For |
|---|---|
| Google Workspace | Large enterprises with Google ecosystem |
| Non-Workspace (Personal Gmail) | Small businesses and lab simulations |

## Implementation Steps
1. Log into ManageEngine dashboard
2. Navigate to **Enrollment** → **Android Enterprise**
3. Click **Configure** → **Register without G Suite**
4. Sign in with dedicated Gmail account
5. Name your organization
6. Complete Google registration
7. Return to ManageEngine — Managed Google Play connected

## App Distribution Methods

| Method | Description |
|---|---|
| Silent Install | Apps pushed automatically — no employee action needed |
| Catalog | Employee chooses from approved app list |
| Email Distribution | App link sent to employee email |

## Business Impact
**Without Managed Google Play:** Employees install 
anything — WhatsApp mods, fake VPNs, malware-infected 
apps — on devices with access to company data.

**With Managed Google Play:** IT controls every app 
on every device. Unauthorized apps cannot be installed.

## Lesson Learned
App management is one of the most underrated MDM 
controls. Most breaches don't start with sophisticated 
hacking — they start with an employee installing 
a malicious app on a device connected to company 
resources.

Controlling the app catalog is controlling the 
attack surface.
